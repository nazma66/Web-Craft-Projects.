What is Mandala?

A mandala is a geometric and often symmetrical design, typically in the form of a circle, that holds deep cultural, spiritual, and artistic significance. The term "mandala" is derived from the ancient Indian language of Sanskrit, meaning "circle" or "disc." Mandalas are found across various cultures and traditions, including Hinduism, Buddhism, Native American spirituality, and ancient Celtic art.
